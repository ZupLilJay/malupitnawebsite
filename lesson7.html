Introduction

The lesson aims to teach about the Internet’s history and importance, with objectives to recount its development and explain cloud computing.

History of the Internet

Timeline spans decades: 1960s (ARPANET launched in 1969), 1970s–1980s (early Internet development), 1990s (WWW, ISPs emerge), 2000s–2010s (expansion), 2020s (cloud computing focus).

Uses of the Internet

Search Information: Almost any content is accessible online.

Digital News: Paperless, unlimited, and easier to access than traditional media.

Communication: Enables real-time messaging and video calls globally.

Data Transfer: Revolutionizes business by speeding up processes that once took days.

Social Networking: Critical for connectivity during the pandemic, making quarantine more manageable.

Internet of Things (IoT)

Connects electronic devices to share data.

Pros: Access info anywhere/anytime, improved device communication, saves time/money, automates tasks.

Cons: Higher hacking risk, challenging data management, system-wide bugs if compromised, no international compatibility standards.

Cloud Computing

Refers to remote network-based services:

Deployment Models: Public (AWS, Azure), Private (OpenStack), Community (FedRAMP), Hybrid (Azure Stack) – each with distinct uses.

​ Service Models: IaaS (infrastructure like VMs), PaaS (app development platforms), SaaS (ready-to-use software), FaaS (event-driven code execution) – with examples like Google Workspace, AWS EC2.

​ Advantages: Scalability, cost efficiency, better management.

​ Disadvantages: Security threats, vendor lock-in, service disruptions.

​ Applications: Used in banking, healthcare, education, government, big data analytics, and more.

Internet Protocols

TCP/IP: Negotiates connections between clients and servers.

​ HTTP: Transmits data on the World Wide Web.

​ FTP: Transfers files between network-connected computers.
