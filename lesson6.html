Introduction

Electronic Media refers to any communication or storage method that requires electronic or electromechanical devices for the audience to access the content. Unlike "static" traditional media (like ink on paper), electronic media relies on electrical signals or digital data to deliver information.

Definitions and Core Concepts

Defines key terms like Media (shapes public awareness), Traditional Media (long-standing channels), Electronic Media (access via electronics), Static Media (no electronics needed in printed form), Frequency (hertz unit for transmission), and Digital Divide (gap in ICT access). Common sources include video/audio recordings, multimedia, CD-ROM, and internet content.

History of Radio

Highlights contributions of Samuel F.B. Morse (Morse Code), Heinrich Hertz (proved wireless transmission), and Guglielmo Marconi (advanced wireless tech). Key milestones: 1916 first radio news bulletin, 1927 established as key info source, and critical role in WWII.

Legal, Ethical, and Societal Issues

Covers issues like copyright, plagiarism, cyberbullying, phishing, and digital piracy. In the Philippines, governed by Intellectual Property laws and the Cybercrime Prevention Act of 2012 (R.A. 10175), which criminalizes illegal access, data/system interference, cybersex, child pornography, identity theft, and online libel.

Significance of Electronic Media

Contributes to information dissemination, communication, education, social change, political engagement, and economic growth.
