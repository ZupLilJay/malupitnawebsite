Introduction

Ethics in the computing industry refers to the moral principles and standards that guide the design, development, use, and management of technology, ensuring it benefits society while minimizing harm.

Privacy and Data Protection

Respecting individuals' right to control their personal information. This includes avoiding unauthorized collection, use, or sharing of data (e.g., from apps, websites, or IoT devices).

Security

Protecting systems, networks, and users from cyber threats. Ethical responsibility involves designing secure products, promptly addressing vulnerabilities, and not using technology to harm others (e.g., hacking, malware).

Accountability

Taking responsibility for the impact of technology. This includes addressing unintended consequences (e.g., social media's role in misinformation) and being transparent about how decisions are made.

Environmental Impact

Minimizing the ecological footprint of computing, from manufacturing hardware to running data centers.
